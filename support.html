Frequently Asked Questions

Does Nightbook send my dreams to a server?

No. Every word you write stays on your device. Voice capture is transcribed on-device by Apple's Speech framework. On iOS 26+ with Apple Intelligence, AI reflections run on your device's Neural Engine. Nothing in the app reaches an external server beyond what you explicitly sync to your own iCloud.

How does iCloud sync work?

If you enable iCloud Sync in Settings → Me → iCloud Sync, your dreams are stored in your private iCloud container through Apple's CloudKit, encrypted end-to-end by Apple. Only your Apple ID can read it. We (TheKnack) have no access. Turn it off and your data stays on the device where you wrote it.

Do I need an account?

No account. No sign-up. Open the app and start writing. iCloud sync uses your existing Apple ID.

What does Nightbook Pro unlock?

The Patterns dashboard, on-device AI reflections (iOS 26+ with Apple Intelligence), unlimited symbol dictionary lookups, custom themes, premium widgets, iCloud sync, and unlimited tags. The journal, voice capture, and all 300 symbols are always free.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are managed by Apple, not by us. Open iPhone Settings → [your name] → Subscriptions → Nightbook → Cancel Subscription. You keep Pro until the end of the current billing period.

Can I get a refund?

Apple handles refunds. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com and select the Nightbook purchase. If Apple declines, email us and we'll see what we can do.

Can I use Nightbook on my iPad and iPhone?

Yes. Install it on both. Enable iCloud sync (Pro) to keep the two in step. On iPad, Nightbook uses a sidebar layout with your journal at full canvas.

Do you support Family Sharing?

Yes. Nightbook Pro (monthly, yearly, and lifetime) is Family Sharing eligible.

Is Nightbook a medical app?

No. Nightbook is a reflective journaling and learning tool, not a medical or diagnostic service. Symbol entries draw from folklore and literature — invitations to reflect, not clinical interpretations. If you are experiencing recurring distressing dreams, please speak with a licensed mental health professional.

Will Nightbook support other languages?

Version 1.0 ships in English only. We want to get the literary voice right in one language before expanding. Additional languages will come in future updates.

Where are my dreams stored on my device?

In Nightbook's local SwiftData store. Voice recordings are kept in the app's Application Support directory. Deleting the app removes all local data. If iCloud sync is on, a copy remains in your private iCloud until you disable sync and remove the container from Settings → [your name] → iCloud.

Troubleshooting

Voice capture isn't transcribing

Check Settings → Nightbook and ensure Microphone and Speech Recognition permissions are on. Transcription requires Apple's on-device Speech framework, which needs a language pack for your locale. If the device has never used Siri in your language, go to Settings → Siri & Search and enable it once — the language pack will download.

Morning reminder isn't firing

Open Settings → Nightbook → Notifications and make sure they're allowed. Then in Nightbook, go to Me → Morning reminder, toggle it off and back on, and pick a time at least 5 minutes in the future for the next scheduled firing.

iCloud sync is stuck

This usually resolves when the device reconnects to Wi-Fi or cellular. Force-quit Nightbook and relaunch. If it still doesn't sync, open Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Nightbook, confirm the container exists, and try again. If the container is missing, sign out and back into iCloud.

I can't find an old entry I'm sure I wrote

Use the Search bar on the Journal tab — it searches the title and body of every entry. If the entry was written on a different device and iCloud sync wasn't enabled, the entry lives only on that device.

App is slow or won't launch

Try force-quitting and relaunching. If the issue persists, back up any important entries (screenshots work in a pinch) and reinstall the app. Reach out if the problem continues — we'll look at it together.
